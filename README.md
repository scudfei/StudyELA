# Alberta ELA Study Resources

A curated set of study guides for Alberta Grade 10–12 English Language Arts (ELA). Each document targets a specific skill area and is built from analysis of actual Alberta Diploma Exam materials.

🌐 **[Visit the Study Resource Website →](https://scudfei.github.io/StudyELA)**

---

## Documents

### 1. [MC Strategies v2 — Multiple Choice Test-Taking Guide](./mc_strategies_v2.md)

A systematic, logic-based strategy guide for the Alberta ELA multiple-choice section. Covers the two types of questions (ordinary vs. best-answer), official strategies from Alberta KEY materials, and targeted approaches for each question type. Includes a quick reference card for exam use.

**Use this when:** approaching any multiple-choice practice session, or when you need a clear decision process for specific question types (vocabulary, tone, inference, literary devices).

---

### 2. [Literary Terms Teaching Guide](./Literary_Terms_Teaching_Guide.md)

Five groups of commonly confused literary concepts, each with verified examples from canonical works and identification strategies. Covers three types of irony, four narrative perspectives, two sonnet structures, four conflict types, and the distinction between satire, parody, and sarcasm.

**Use this when:** preparing for literary device identification questions, or when you need to distinguish between closely related terms such as dramatic irony vs. situational irony, or omniscient vs. limited omniscient narration.

---

### 3. [ELA Training & Exam Guide](./ELA_Training_and_Exam_Guide.md)

A two-part framework built from analysis of 65 wrong answers. Part 1 covers five daily training habits designed to become automatic through repeated practice. Part 2 covers four in-exam decision rules — each with a single trigger and a single action, all under 30 seconds.

**Use this when:** setting up a daily practice routine, or when you need clear and fast rules for exam-day decision-making.

---

### 4. [Alberta ELA Terminology Study Guide](./Alberta_ELA_Terminology_Study_Guide.md)

A comprehensive reference covering all major Alberta ELA term categories: text understanding, tone and mood, literary devices, characterisation, poetry forms, grammar, and exam task terminology. Includes definitions, examples, and identification strategies.

**Use this when:** you encounter an unfamiliar term in practice materials, or need a complete term list to review before a test.

---

### 5. [ELA Writing Logic Framework](./ELA_Writing_Logic_Framework.md)

A structured approach to Alberta ELA writing tasks — Personal Response to Texts (PRT) and formal essays. Grounded in actual Alberta curriculum materials, with explicit formulas for thesis construction, paragraph structure, and evidence integration.

**Use this when:** preparing for written response assignments, or when your writing is structured but lacks a clear central argument.

---

## How to Start

If you are new to this resource, begin with **MC Strategies v2** for an overview of how Alberta ELA multiple-choice questions work. Then use the **ELA Training & Exam Guide** to understand your specific error patterns and which habit to build first. Use the **Literary Terms Guide** and **Terminology Study Guide** as reference when specific knowledge gaps appear. The **Writing Framework** applies specifically to written response tasks.
